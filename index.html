    Opening Balances represent the financial snapshot of the organization at the beginning of a fiscal period or when implementing the ERP system.     • Assets: Record the opening balances for assets such as cash, accounts receivable, inventory, and fixed assets.
    • Liabilities: Record the opening balances for liabilities including accounts payable, loans, and accrued expenses.

    In eresource On-Demand, Opening Balances serve as the foundation for accurate financial management and reporting.

  • Balance Sheet
    Balance Sheet

    "Balancing the Numbers, Charting Your Success"

    The Balance Sheet, also known as the Statement of Financial Position, is a financial report that displays an organization's assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity as of a specific date.The Balance Sheet provides insights into an organization's financial health, liquidity, and net worth.

     Assets: List the organization's assets, including current assets (e.g. cash, accounts receivable) and non-current assets (e.g. property, equipment).

     Liabilities: Enumerate the organization's liabilities, such as current liabilities (e.g. accounts payable) and long-term liabilities (e.g. loans).

     Shareholders' Equity: Display the shareholders' equity, which includes retained earnings and capital contributions.

    You can analyze the Balance Sheet to understand the financial standing of the organization.

    Examine the relationship between assets, liabilities, and equity to assess liquidity and solvency.
